Route your wiring diagram and program. Ask for help at #THWIP on Slack.

-2

Ship it!

Submit your repository with a breakdown of your design. We'll review it, and if everything looks solid, we'll ship your THWIP!! hardware kit right to your door.

Get your package in the mail! Wire the MPU6050 and your extra components to your XIAO ESP32-C3 using the tiny breadboard to test it, or directly solder it if you're super sure it works! Upload your code and strap it to your wrist.

FAQ

What exactly is an HID?

HID stands for Human Interface Device. It's the standard USB protocol that keyboards, mice, and gamepads use to talk to a computer. Because the XIAO ESP32-C3 supports native USB, you can program it to act exactly like a standard keyboard or mouse—no extra drivers needed!

All this, for free?!

Yes! If your initial design and code get approved, we'll send you the core hardware kit completely free. In exchange, you have to assemble it, document your code, and ship your final high-quality project.

Are there any requirements to get approved?

For your project to be considered for approval, you'll need to add at least two extra components from the THWIP!! kit into your design, otherwise it will be rejected. But don't worry, you'll still have the opportunity to resubmit again once you update it!

Can I map the gestures to do anything?

Because it acts as a standard USB HID, the computer thinks it's a keyboard and mouse. You can code a wrist flick to be "Scroll Down", or to be "Left Click", or an insanely complex keyboard macro.
